Calicut International Airport — Ground Handling SLA
CONFIDENTIAL
GROUND HANDLING SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT
Calicut International Airport (CCJ)
This Service Level Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into between Calicut International Airport Authority
(“Airport”) and the ground handling service providers named herein (each a “Vendor”), effective from 01 April
2026 to 31 December 2026 (“Term”).
1. Parties to the Agreement
1.1 Airport
Calicut International Airport Authority, Kozhikode, Kerala, India.
1.2 Vendors
- SkyServe Ground Handling Pvt Ltd — Baggage Handling Services
- AeroClean Facility Services — Aircraft Cabin Cleaning Services
- FuelTech Aviation Services — Aircraft Fueling Services
2. Scope of Services
Each Vendor shall provide the ground handling service(s) designated to it under Section 1.2 for all scheduled
and chartered flights operating at the Airport during the Term, in accordance with the performance standards set
out in Sections 3 through 5 below.

6. Breach Detection and Reporting
- Each Vendor shall submit daily operational performance data to the Airport's monitoring system by 23:59 on
the day of operation, recording the actual start time and stop time of each event listed in Sections 3–5.
- A “breach” occurs whenever the elapsed time between the recorded start event and stop event for a given
metric exceeds the maximum duration benchmark specified in the applicable Service Level Standards section
(Sections 3–5) for that metric.
- Each breach shall be logged with the associated flight number, date, recorded start time, recorded stop time,
and the specific clause breached.
- The Airport shall notify the relevant Vendor of any breach within 24 hours of detection.
- Vendors may raise a formal dispute against any logged breach within 7 calendar days of notification,
accompanied by supporting evidence.
7. Penalty Invoicing
- Penalties for breaches shall accrue per occurrence as specified in the applicable Service Level Standards
section.
- The Airport shall issue a consolidated penalty invoice to each Vendor on a monthly basis, itemizing every
breach, the associated flight number and date, and the penalty amount.
- Undisputed penalty invoices are payable within 15 days of issuance.
8. Compliance Scoring
Each Vendor's compliance score shall be calculated as: (Total Service Events - Breach Events) / Total Service
Events × 100, evaluated monthly and on a rolling basis over the Term.
Rating Score Range Basis Action
Green — Compliant >= 95% Rolling compliance score No action required
Yellow — At Risk 80% – 94.9% Rolling compliance score Performance improvement notice issued
Red — Non-Compliant < 80% Rolling compliance score Formal review; renewal at risk
9. Contract Renewal
Not later than 90 days prior to expiry of the Term, the Airport shall review each Vendor's trailing 12-month (or,
where the Term is shorter, the full available period) compliance score and shall determine, in its sole discretion,
whether to (a) renew the Agreement on existing terms, (b) renew subject to renegotiated terms, or (c) decline to
renew and seek an alternative service provider.

10. Force Majeure
No penalty shall accrue for any breach directly caused by force majeure events, including but not limited to
severe weather, air traffic control strikes, security incidents, or government-mandated operational restrictions,
provided the affected Vendor notifies the Airport within 24 hours of the event.
11. Term and Termination
This Agreement shall remain in effect from 01 April 2026 through 31 December 2026, unless terminated earlier
by either party for material breach upon 30 days' written notice, or extended by mutual written agreement of the
parties.
